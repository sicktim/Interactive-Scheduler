Course Naming and Numbering Convention 
Most event titles begin with the platform name (e.g., C-12, F-16, T-38, GROOT, etc.) where applicable. Additionally, when a particular event 
does not apply to the entire class, the subset of students to whom it applies is denoted in parenthesis at the end of the block title (e.g., ABM, CSO, 
FTE, STC, etc.). Finally, the course number designation follows the convention below. The overall program is broken into multiple phases. Each 
phase consists of courses indicated by the first digit. Each course consists of learning modules, which trace to a specific set of terminal learning 
objectives (TLOs). A module consists of blocks of learning which are grouped by topic. A block may be composed of multiple units and include 
the enabling learning objectives (ELOs) which achieve the learning required by the module’s TLOs. Sequential blocks indicate a required order of 
instruction (e.g., Block 1, Block 2, Block 3) while a numerical break in the block numbering (e.g., Block 10, Block 20, Block 30) indicates no 
required sequence unless otherwise listed within the prerequisites. The last letter indicates the type of event.

Event Descriptions 
This section describes the various event types which the school uses to execute the curriculum. 
Academic Lecture 
The foundational principles of the curriculum are introduced in academic lectures. Students will be given the 
opportunity to learn theoretical concepts necessary to participate in hands-on events. Students will also be given basic 
principles behind the techniques required to interpret the data they acquire for various assignments. 
Asynchronous Content 
This event type includes content delivered as computer-based training (CBT) or content that students can view in a 
self-paced manner such as prerecorded lectures. 
Control Room 
A control room is a room which typically provides individual workstations for test conductors, a test director, and 
various discipline engineers (e.g., propulsion, aerodynamics, avionics) arranged in tiered rows and displaying real-time 
telemetry, video feeds, and communication channels. This setup facilitates immediate access to critical data and provides 
direct communication with the operators during flight or space tests. The control room personnel are responsible for 
overseeing test execution and enhancing test safety. 
Exams/Assessments 
Exams and assessments are used to assess student learning in relation to the TLOs and ELOs throughout the course. 
They are typically based on individual performance and are conducted in class or in a take-home format as determined by 
the applicable instructors. 
Flight and Space System Operation Events 
Flight and Space System Operation Events will reinforce the principles introduced in the classroom, laboratory, and 
simulation environments, enabling students to see those principles demonstrated in a relevant environment. Students 
should be able to readily identify the applicable principles during the event. Students should also be able to apply the 
knowledge acquired to interpret in real-time the different events during the event. The data collected during these events 
will be analyzed based on the previously learned concepts. Since T&E is reliant on effective teams, many of these events 
will be performed in groups.  
Ground School 
Ground school is conducted for each aircraft or space system that the students will operate. This instruction covers the 
system description, normal and emergency procedures, and any applicable operating limits to ensure safe operations. This 
training is conducted by an instructor of the applicable system.  
Ground Training 
Ground training includes several events required for safe operation in the flight or space environment but is typically 
conducted by experts in the applicable operation such as survival or aircrew flight equipment. This training supports the 
operation of flight and space systems, but in contrast to the ground school is not usually conducted by the instructor of the 
actual aircraft or satellite.  
Ground Test 
Ground test includes all events where test cards are executed but flight or operation is not planned. These events 
typically occur prior to a flight or space test event.  
Laboratories and Simulators 
The laboratories and simulators are considered part of the academics for grading and instructional purposes. These 
events are where principles introduced in the classroom portion are demonstrated and reinforced. Students should have a 
firm grasp of the principles outlined in these exercises by the time they complete the events. Students will be given hands 
on opportunities to collect data as well as analyze the collected data. Focused individual instruction can also occur on an 
as-needed basis. 
Mission Information Briefs (MIBs) 
These briefings are conducted to explain the details of a flight or space operation event. They are often accompanied 
by a mission planning guide (MPG) which aids the students and instructors in leading the execution event and ensures 
alignment with the applicable ELOs for the flight or space event.

Reports 
The culmination of most flight and space system operation events are the reports that are used to disseminate the 
information to the rest of the test community. The reports will often require taking data from individual events and 
making conclusions supported by analysis. Written and Oral reports will be required throughout the curriculum as part of 
both case studies and at the conclusion of practical exercises. Reports will often require data from tests to support 
conclusions and recommendations resulting from student analysis. Depending on the event, the reporting products may be 
an informal oral report, an informal written report, a formal written report, a formal oral report, or a combination thereof. 
Reporting requirements will be identified as part of each event brief. Reports generally will be graded. 
Working Groups 
Working groups include several events where students collaborate both internal and external to Test Pilot School. The 
working groups are often used to facilitate test team collaboration in preparation for upcoming test events.  
Debriefs 
At the conclusion of selected blocks debrief will be conducted to address any areas of missed student learning and 
collect feedback from student surveys for future course improvement.
