Check Flight Training 
The prerequisite Check Flight (CF) Training prepares FTC and STC students to safely serve as crewmembers in 
curriculum aircraft and spacecraft. Test pilot students are qualified as crew solo aircraft commanders in selected 
curriculum aircraft, including the T-38, the C-12, or F-16 for pilots assigned to one of those performance data groups. 
Additionally, select pilots will also receive crew solo qualification in the SGS 2-33 Glider. Pilots receive a familiarization 
sortie in the F-16 and/or C-12 if they are not in one of those data groups. ABMs, CSOs, and FTEs are provided hands-on 
experience as aircrew members in the C-172 through the Airmanship curriculum. ABMs, CSOs, and FTEs are introduced 
to advanced aircraft operations and data collection tasks through data aircraft familiarization sorties in the T-38, C-12, F-
16, and Gliders. RPA pilots accomplish a cross section of sorties in the C-172, T-38, C-12, F-16, and Gliders to develop 
basic proficiency in manned aircraft operations and provide curriculum aircraft familiarization. ABMs, CSOs, and RPA 
pilots will also receive a crew solo qualification in the ground control station of the LJ-25. All FTC students receive 
exposure to formation flight. The CF Training phase also provides familiarization training designed to prepare students for 
specific curriculum flight test sorties including familiarization with F-16 characteristics during departures from controlled 
flight. STC students will receive a limited exposure to a selection of the curriculum aircraft and receive the requisite 
ground training for those aircraft. 
Faculty Contact Information 
The faculty for this phase consists primarily of the Chief Test Pilot, Director of Flight Operations, Training Officer, 
and the Chief Pilots for the various aircraft. 
Note on Grades 
The CF Training phase provides the training and qualification necessary to start the related academic content. 
Although most events in the CF phase are graded, these grades are only used to document that the student has achieved 
the required learning objectives and proficiency levels. Failure to achieve the required proficiency level would normally 
prevent the student from progressing in the curriculum and necessitate removal from the course. There are no semester 
hours associated with this Phase. CF grades do not factor into GPA, but with very few exceptions all events must be 
completed or passed to complete the USAF TPS course. 
References and Texts 
Most courses will be taught in class from PowerPoint presentations. These presentations will generally be available to 
the students on the TPS curriculum portal. The CF phase is based almost entirely on aircraft flight manuals, Air Force 
Instructions, and local area procedures (including relevant FAA policies). Most references will be available on-line either 
through the Curriculum Portal or Center Ops Online (COOL). If the student has access to an “Electronic Flight Bag” 
(EFB) digital notepad, most documents will be available there as well. 
Phase Organization and Learning Objectives 
The CF Phase is organized into 2 major courses: CF 5000 Common Ground Training and CF 6000 Flight Training. 
Achievement of a required skill may be evaluated through formal proficiency testing, face-to-face evaluation, and 
simulator or flight grades. Students do not receive formal qualification (i.e. a “Form 8” or check ride) in any event or 
aircraft but there are numerous events that must be successfully completed to pass the CF phase and continue into the 
curriculum. An overview can be seen in Figure 4.
