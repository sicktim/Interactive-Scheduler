Test and Evaluation Curriculum  
The following sections contain descriptions for all courses and events for the Astronautical Sciences 
Phase (STC Only), Flight Sciences Phases (FTC Only), Mission Systems Phase, Test Foundations Phase, 
and Test Leadership Phase.  
Astronautical Sciences Phase 
In this phase, students will develop a deeper understanding of the technical and engineering aspects 
and the considerations for T&E of space systems. Initial material will provide a baseline understanding of 
T&E considerations for space systems and will build during the second half of the module which will 
provide a greater level of detailed astronautical knowledge. A portion of the second half of this phase will 
be taught by instructors from the USAFA Astronautics Department. Case studies and guest lectures and 
homework assignments will reinforce material presented in the lectures. Concepts are further reinforced 
with a practical exercise conducted with rockets in which a test event is planned, executed, and reported 
on by the student with coaching from instructors. Additional hands-on events will include multiple lab, 
FlatSat, and on-orbit exercises. An overview can be seen in Figure 12, where the 6000 level course may 
be considered Fundamentals, while the 7000 level course include the systems needed, and the 8000 level 
incudes advanced graduate treatments. Meanwhile, the 9000 level includes classified content, Practitioner 
content and trial versions of candidate new courses.

AS 9200 Space Threats 
Terminal Learning Objectives:  
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to: 
TLO 1. Recall critical concepts of space threats.  
 
AS 9201A Threats to Space Systems Lecture 
AS 9202E Threats to Space Systems Assessment 
AS 9203Z Threats to Space Systems Debrief 
Lectures cover the five major threat threads of Co-orbital Anti-Satellites (ASAT), direct ascent 
ASAT, Directed Energy Weapons (DEW), Cyber Attack, and Ground Infrastructure Attack. This course 
will consist of a series of classified lectures and will discuss adversary threats with the potential to restrict 
US freedom of action in space and disrupt, deny, degrade, and destroy space assets providing critical 
capabilities to the warfighter. Synthesis event where students will perform a threat surface analysis of a 
space system against a specific threat thread and brief it to the block. Students will be assessed on their 
threat analysis and presentation. 
Prerequisites: 
AN 5150A Astronautical Phase Introduction

Terminal Learning Objectives:  
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to:

AS 9901A Space Shuttle Proximity Maneuvering - Guest Lecture   
This lecture will be taught in an unclassified setting as a guest lecture and will provide a description 
of the rendezvous process and issues associated with close proximity maneuvering with another orbiting 
object. This includes the description of the space shuttle aircraft and MIR, the role of the space shuttle 
pilot in rendezvous and close proximity maneuvering, and provides essential lessons learned from 
extended close proximity operations. 
Prerequisites: 
AS 8201A Rendezvous & Proximity Ops Planning Lecture
