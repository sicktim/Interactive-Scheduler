Test Leadership Phase 
The Test Leadership phase of the curriculum is designed to equip students with the skills and 
knowledge necessary to effectively lead test and evaluation programs. This section focuses on strategic 
thinking, communication, negotiation, innovation, and the application of lessons learned from real-world

TL 5000 Strategic Context 
(0 Semester Credit Hours) 
This course provides an overview of the strategic context of test and evaluation, including the role of 
the US military in the global security environment, the importance of test and evaluation in supporting 
military operations, and the key stakeholders and organizations involved in the test and evaluation 
process. Students will learn about the unique strategic contexts of various Combatant Commands 
(COCOMs) and their implications for test and evaluation, as well as the technological capabilities and 
limitations of US and adversary forces. The course will also cover the basics of the DoD acquisition 
system and the role of test and evaluation in supporting the development and fielding of new military 
systems.  
TL 5100 Strategic Context 
Terminal Learning Objectives: 
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to: 
TLO 1. Describe how S&T works with the Test Enterprise. 
TLO 2. Identify the primary missions of S&T organizations like AFRL and DARPA in the context 
of defense innovation. 
TLO 3. Recognize and explain the unique strategic contexts of various COCOMs and their 
implications for test and evaluation. 
TLO 4. Describe the key components and processes of the DoD acquisition system, including the 
roles and responsibilities of various stakeholders, and explain how they impact test and 
evaluation activities. 
TLO 5. Compare and contrast the technological capabilities and limitations of US and adversary 
forces. 
TLO 6. Describe the basic security protocols for testing sensitive technologies. 
TLO 7. Identify the security boundaries and program objectives of the TPS SAP baseline. 
TLO 8. Define key Operational Test terminology, including Operational Effectiveness and 
Suitability. 
TLO 9. Differentiate between the objectives of Developmental Test (DT) and Operational Test 
(OT). 
 
TL 5101A Intro to Science and Technology (S&T) 
This section explores the ecosystem of defense innovation, focusing on the critical transition of 
technology from the laboratory to the warfighter ("crossing the Valley of Death"). Students will examine 
the specific roles and missions of key S&T organizations, such as the Air Force Research Lab (AFRL) 
and the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA). The course also investigates the impact 
of disruptive emerging technologies—such as Collaborative Combat Aircraft (CCAs) and Long Range 
Kill Chains (LRKC)—on future test ranges and methodologies. 
Prerequisites: 
AN 5190A Test Leadership Phase Intro 
 
TL 5102A Combatant Command Perspectives 
This block provides a comparative analysis of the strategic environments facing major geographic and 
functional Combatant Commands, with specific focus on EUCOM, INDOPACOM, SPACECOM, and 
CYBERCOM. Students will investigate how the unique geopolitical landscapes, physical environments, 
and adversary capabilities within each Area of Responsibility (AOR) drive specific requirements for 
system performance and test evaluation.

TL 5103A Intro to Acquisition 
This course section provides a foundational understanding of the Department of Defense acquisition 
framework. It covers the interaction between the Joint Capabilities Integration and Development System 
(JCIDS), the Defense Acquisition System, and the Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution 
(PPBE) process. Special emphasis is placed on the "V-model" of systems engineering and the Tester’s 
role in influencing requirements early in the lifecycle to ensure programs are structurally sound for 
eventual fielding. 
Prerequisites: 
AN 5190A Test Leadership Phase Intro 
 
TL 5104A Intelligence Briefs 
This module addresses the critical intersection of intelligence, security, and test execution. Students 
will receive necessary security indoctrination and gain insight into the capabilities of national intelligence 
assets. This will include briefs to cover technological capabilities and limitations of US and adversary 
forces.  
Prerequisites: 
AN 5190A Test Leadership Phase Intro 
 
TL 5105A Special Access Program (SAP) Briefs 
This section includes the formal indoctrination to the TPS Special Access Program (SAP) student 
baseline. Students will complete mandatory security training and receive detailed overviews of each 
program within the baseline to ensure full compliance and situational awareness for subsequent secure 
curriculum events. 
Prerequisites: 
AN 5190A Test Leadership Phase Intro 
 
TL 5110A Introduction to Operational Test 
This block introduces students to formal Operational Test (OT) in the context of fielding new 
capabilities. An overview of the US laws governing OT and independent operational assessments will be 
presented. OT objectives and test considerations will be compared with those of Developmental Test 
(DT). Key OT terminology such as Effectiveness and Suitability will be presented, in addition to the 
different OT organizations and considerations for integrated testing will be discussed. 
Prerequisites: 
AN 5190A Test Leadership Phase Intro

TL 6000 Negotiation, Persuasion, & Communication (NPC) 
(1 Semester Credit Hours) 
This course focuses on the skills and knowledge required to negotiate, persuade, and communicate 
effectively in a test and evaluation context. Students will learn about the principles and practices of 
negotiation, including the seven elements of negotiation, the negotiator's toolbox, and the psychology of 
persuasion. The course will also cover the importance of effective communication in test and evaluation, 
including critical communication skills, group negotiations, and negotiating remotely.  
TL 6100 Negotiation, Persuasion, & Communication (NPC) 
Terminal Learning Objectives: 
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to: 
TLO 1. Apply effective negotiation strategies to resolve test-related conflicts and achieve mutually 
beneficial outcomes. 
TLO 2. Develop persuasive communication skills to influence stakeholders and advocate for test 
priorities, resources, and requirements. 
TLO 3. Analyze the interests, needs, and motivations of various test stakeholders, including 
government agencies, contractors, and military personnel, to inform negotiation and 
persuasion approaches  
 
TL 6101A Negotiation, Persuasion, & Communication (NPC) Course 
TL 6102Y Oral Report 
This is a week-long block taught by instructors outside of TPS. Through a combination of lectures, 
discussions, and exercises, students will develop the skills and confidence needed to negotiate and 
communicate effectively with stakeholders, including program offices, contractors, and military 
personnel. 
Prerequisites: 
AN 5190A Test Leadership Phase Intro

TL 7000 Innovation 
(2 Semester Credit Hours) 
This course is designed to help students develop the skills and knowledge required to innovate and 
improve test and evaluation processes and systems. Students will learn about the principles and practices 
of innovation, including design thinking, lean startup, and agile methodologies. The course will cover the 
importance of innovation in test and evaluation, including the need for continuous improvement and the 
role of innovation in supporting the development and fielding of new military systems. This course also 
introduces the students to data-driven testing which is built upon in other phases.  
TL 7100 Innovation 
Terminal Learning Objectives: 
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to: 
TLO 1. Apply innovation frameworks to develop and pitch innovative solutions that address 
complex test and evaluation challenges. 
TLO 2. Utilize innovation processes and strategies to design and develop new test methods, tools, 
and technologies that improve test efficiency, effectiveness, and quality. 
TLO 3. Collaborate with peers and instructors to refine and iterate on innovation projects. 
TLO 4. Demonstrate the ability to adapt and pivot in response to changing conditions and 
requirements. 
 
TL 7101A Innovation Course 
TL 7102Y Student Team Presentations in Class 
TL 7103Y Pitch Day Presentation 
This is a week-long innovation block taught by instructors out of TPS. Through a combination of 
lectures, discussions, and exercises, students will develop the skills and knowledge needed to apply 
innovation principles and practices to real-world test and evaluation challenges. 
Prerequisites: 
AN 5190A Test Leadership Phase Intro

TL 7200 Data-Driven Test Introduction  
Terminal Learning Objectives: 
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to: 
TLO 1. Apply fundamental Python programming concepts (variables, data types, logic, loops, and 
functions) to solve structured coding problems. 
TLO 2. Apply common Python libraries to import and manipulate data, establishing a software 
foundation for subsequent coursework in robotic perception, planning, and control. 
TLO 3. Explain fundamental AI/ML concepts, the components of an AI pipeline, and the role of 
data-driven methodologies in modern test and evaluation. 
TLO 4. Discuss the importance of verification, validation, and explainability (V&V/X) for AI-
enabled systems and summarize the ethical considerations relevant to military applications. 
TLO 5. Apply data preprocessing techniques to structure and prepare a sample dataset for model 
training. 
TLO 6. Implement a basic machine learning model within a hands-on lab environment and 
demonstrate the process of testing its performance against defined metrics. 
TLO 7. Identify real-world applications of AI/ML technologies presented during industry tours and 
relate them to potential military use cases. 
TLO 8. Describe the practical challenges and opportunities of technology implementation as 
observed in operational environments at various technology companies.

data types, functions, logic, loops and working with popular external libraires. Learning these basics will 
enable you to complete hands on assignments in robotic perception, planning, and control. This block will 
also provide a foundation to understand the software driving modern air and space technologies.

TPS Enrollment

The students will participate in a 2-week trip to MIT or Standford which will provide a detailed 
overview of Data-Driven Test techniques and visits to industry.  This immersive trip, a collaboration 
between the USAF Test Pilot School and a leading academic institution like MIT or Stanford, provides 
students with a comprehensive introduction to Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) for 
test and evaluation. The trip consists of academic instruction from university faculty on core topics, 
including AI fundamentals, data management, compute infrastructure, multimodal AI, and cybersecurity. 
A significant portion of the trip is dedicated to hands-on application through lab work, culminating in a 
capstone challenge. The experience is further enhanced by immersing students in the practical application 
of AI through tours of local technology companies and defense-focused labs, reinforcing the real-world 
relevance of the concepts learned. The overall goal is to equip future test professionals with the 
foundational knowledge and practical skills to design, evaluate, and implement AI/ML systems in 
complex operational environments.  
Prerequisites:

TL 8000 Case Studies 
(1 Semester Credit Hours) 
This course provides students with the opportunity to analyze and discuss real-world case studies of 
test and evaluation programs, including the successes and challenges faced by these programs. Students 
will learn about the key stakeholders, organizations, and processes involved in test and evaluation, as well 
as the importance of effective leadership, communication, and collaboration in supporting the success of 
these programs. 
TL 8100 Case Studies 
Terminal Learning Objectives: 
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to: 
TLO 1. Identify and describe the roles, interests, and perspectives of various stakeholders involved 
in test and acquisition programs, including program offices, requirements, operational test, 
developmental test, contractors, and other relevant entities. 
TLO 2. Analyze and discuss the strategic and operational contexts of specific test and acquisition 
programs, including the impact of technological advancements, budget constraints, and 
changing operational requirements on program decisions and outcomes. 
TLO 3. Evaluate programmatic risk management approaches and perspectives and discuss the 
implications of risk management decisions on test and acquisition outcomes. 
TLO 4. Extract lessons learned from case studies of various test and acquisition programs to 
develop a deeper understanding of the complexities and challenges of test leadership and to 
inform future decision-making. 
TLO 5. Make and defend recommendations for improving test and acquisition programs, including 
the identification of opportunities for innovation, the mitigation of risks, and the 
optimization of resources. 
 
TL 8101A Case Study Seminar 1 
TL 8102Y Case Study Report 1 
TL 8110-70A Case Study Seminar 2-8 
TL 8111-71Y Case Study Report 2-8 
Eight full day Case Study Seminars occur at a rate of about once a month. Students will develop the 
skills and knowledge needed to apply the lessons learned from these case studies to real-world test and 
evaluation challenges, including the ability to analyze complex problems, identify key issues and 
opportunities, and develop effective solutions. 
Prerequisites: 
AN 5190A Test Leadership Phase Intro
