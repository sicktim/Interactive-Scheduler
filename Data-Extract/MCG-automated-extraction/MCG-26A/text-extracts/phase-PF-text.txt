Flight Sciences: Performance Phase 
The first primary goal of the Performance Phase is to learn the theory and methods of evaluating 
aircraft climb, cruise, acceleration, turn, propulsion, descent and landing performance. Teams of students 
collaborate in executing a limited performance flight test project in one of the available data aircraft (C-
12, T-38, F-16D, and Learjet). Data collected from such flights are reduced, analyzed and presented in 
written reports and a formal briefing. 
The second primary goal of the Performance Phase (in conjunction with the Test Foundations Phase) 
is to teach and practice methods for efficiently planning, briefing, conducting, and reporting on airborne 
tests. Emphasis is placed on appropriate and efficient communications before and during flight with the 
test conductor in the aircraft with the test pilot. Principles of Cockpit Resource Management (CRM) are 
emphasized. Students are evaluated on their test conduct skills as a major portion of their grade on the 
Performance Final Practical Exam. 
References and Texts 
Most courses will be taught in class from PowerPoint presentations. These presentations will 
generally be available to the students on the TPS curriculum portal. Some course textbooks are available 
on the portal in electronic format. The following textbooks will be issued to the students and turned in

Terminal Learning Objectives:

PF 7200 Propulsion 
Terminal Learning Objectives: 
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to: 
TLO 1. Describe the fundamental principles of propulsion and aircraft engine design, including the 
core components, and performance characteristics. 
TLO 2. Discuss the function and design considerations of key engine components including inlets, 
compressors, fans, combustors, turbines, afterburners, and nozzles.  
TLO 3. Calculate turbine engine performance using cycle analysis techniques, comparing ideal and 
real engine cycles, and produce key performance parameters such as thrust, specific thrust, 
and thrust-specific fuel consumption.  
TLO 4. Assess engine operability, stability, and control characteristics, recognizing performance 
trends, testing methodologies, and safety considerations in engine operation, including 
during ground and flight testing.  
TLO 5. Examine emerging propulsion technologies and systems, including adaptive cycle engines, 
rotating detonation engines, and integrated hybrid propulsion systems, and predict their 
implications for future propulsion applications.  
 
PF 7201A Propulsion Lecture 
PF 7202E Propulsion Exam 
PF 7203Z Propulsion Debrief 
These blocks cover the fundamentals of propulsion with an emphasis on flight test applications. It 
begins with first-principles analysis and applies them to various propulsion types, with a primary focus on 
turbine engines. Key topics include engine components, cycle analysis, performance evaluation, 
operability, and control characteristics. Students will assess propulsion system limitations, failure modes, 
and advanced concepts, including emerging technologies and alternative propulsion methods. The course 
prepares students to analyze, evaluate, and test propulsion systems in a flight test environment using 
analytical methods and practical applications. 
Prerequisites: 
PF 6111E Compressible/Hypersonic Aerodynamics Exam
