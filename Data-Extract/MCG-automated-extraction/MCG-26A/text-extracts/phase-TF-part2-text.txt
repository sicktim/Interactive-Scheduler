TF 6300 Analyze 
Terminal Learning Objectives: 
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to: 
TLO 1. Apply statistical techniques. 
 
TF 6301A Statistics for Testers Lecture 
TF 6302Y Statistics for Testers Oral Report 
This block will teach students how to use statistical techniques to extract information from test data. 
Specifically, the role that statistics can play in the test process is discussed. The following basic concepts 
are explained: variability, random sampling, designed experiments, mechanistic versus empirical models, 
enumerative versus analytic studies, and finally sample mean and variance versus population mean and 
variance. This block also teaches students to compute various statistics, display results, and interpret 
them. It covers the basics of probability distributions and how to use them. Finally, students will be able 
to apply the following basic statistical techniques to test problems: hypothesis tests, linear regression, 
confidence intervals, and prediction intervals. 
Prerequisites: 
TF 6101A PEARS Introduction Lecture 
 
TF 6310A Statistics Based Test Design Lecture 
TF 6311Y Statistics Based Test Design Oral Report 
TF 6312Z Statistics Based Test Design Debrief 
Building on the statistics learned and reviewed in TF 6301A, this block will teach students how to 
apply statistical and constrained optimization methods to designing statistically defensible tests while 
meeting programmatic constraints such as test hours, instrumentation costs, and total budget. The general 
concept of Design of Experiments (DOE) is derived using optimization theory and applied to the concept 
of designing a constrained test program that can answer the customer’s questions in a statistically 
defensible manner.  
Prerequisites: 
TF 6301A Statistics for Testers Lecture 
 
TF 6320A Data Presentation Lecture 
This block covers techniques for presenting data in a variety of media to communicate a clear 
message to the intended audience. Methods of presenting data in a visible, clear, and convincing manner 
are covered along with common presentation pitfalls. 
Prerequisites: 
TF 6101A PEARS Introduction Lecture

TF 6500 Special Topics 
Terminal Learning Objectives: 
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to: 
TLO 1. Recall critical elements of special test foundations topics. 
 
TF 6501A Space Acquisition and Test Lecture (STC) 
TF 6502A Space Acquisition and Test Lecture (US Only STC) 
This block introduces students to space acquisition/test and identifies the similarities and differences 
between space and air vehicle test. The student is introduced to the space environment, systems, missions, 
organizations, acquisition, and test. The block includes a discussion of space policy guidance and specific 
discussions of space developmental and operational test, including examples and real-world vignettes. 
This block also introduces classified space systems with a discussion of real-world acquisition and test 
considerations for such systems.  
Prerequisites: 
TF 6101A PEARS Introduction Lecture  
 
TF 6510A Joint Test and Evaluation (JT&E) (STC) 
This is a two day-long guided exercise provided by the Air Force Joint Test Program Office. The 
students will exercise the process that rapidly provides non-materiel solutions to identified operational 
deficiencies. The course will review operational test definitions such as Critical Operational Issues (COI), 
System Under Test (SUT), and Tactics, Techniques and Procedures (TTP), and Measures of 
Effectiveness/Suitability (MOE/MOS). The students will be posed with operational deficiencies and will 
generate a COI, and associated MOEs/MOSs. The students will develop an execution/data plan for a “test 
event” of a non-materiel solution (TTP). They will analyze the results and report on the findings. 
Prerequisites: 
TF 6101A PEARS Introduction Lecture 
 
TF 6520A All Weather Testing Lecture 
This block teaches students why all-weather testing is done, what the major objectives are, where 
these tests are conducted, and how to plan and execute an all-weather test. 
Prerequisites:  
 
TF 6101A PEARS Introduction Lecture

 TF 7000 Qualitative Evaluations 
(3 Semester Credit Hours) 
The Qualitative Evaluation (qual eval, QE) program is designed to build a strong and broad 
foundation of experience for test aircrew through exposure to different aircraft, systems, and missions 
using a variety of military and civilian platforms. This program will reinforce the TPS curriculum through 
application of theory, test techniques, analysis, and reporting. It will expose TPS students to a variety of 
aircraft performance, flying qualities, and systems characteristics while increasing confidence in their 
ability to handle new test situations in a systematic and logical manner using a buildup approach. 
TF 7100 Graded Qualitative Evaluations 
Terminal Learning Objectives: 
Upon completion of this Module, students will be able to: 
TLO 1. Differentiate between a variety of aircraft/spacecraft performance, flying qualities, and 
systems characteristics as their experiences broaden through exposure to multiple platforms.  
TLO 2. Prioritize collection of appropriate, mission-relevant data which will answer evaluation 
objectives during planning and execution of single-sortie qualitative evaluations in 
unfamiliar platforms.  
TLO 3. Interpret qualitative evaluation results in the context of system requirements and the 
platforms’ intended mission through limited-scope written reports. 
 
TF 7101A Qual Eval Program Intro Lecture 
TF 7102A Contractor Interaction Lecture 
Students are introduced to the purpose of qualitative evaluations. Techniques are presented to help the 
students make the most of every qual eval, and specific safety considerations are introduced. The lesson 
emphasizes techniques on how to properly report on their flight and gives guidelines for the graded qual 
eval flights. Since many of the qual eval instructor pilots will be contractors, students are reminded of the 
sensitivities faced when dealing with contractors and are instructed on the proper way to interface with 
them. 
Prerequisites: 
AN 5121A Dean of Faculty Introduction 
 
TF 7110M Graded Qual Eval 1 MIB 
TF 7111G Graded Qual Eval 1 Ground School 
TF 7112F Graded Qual Eval 1 Flight 
TF 7113R Graded Qual Eval 1 Report 
For each of the three graded qual eval flights, an MIB will introduce the aircraft and the mission for 
which it will be evaluated. The instructor pilot will provide ground school academics covering the major 
systems of the subject aircraft as well as any required briefing items and aircraft handling techniques. 
Following each qual eval flight, students will write a qual eval report documenting their flight and 
evaluating the aircraft for use in a particular mission. 
Prerequisites: 
FQ 6222F Learjet Lat-Dir Stability Flight (P/RPA) [req’d for TF 7112F] 
FQ 6223F Learjet Combined Long/Lat-Dir Flight (ABM/CSO/FTE) [req’d for TF 7112F] 
TF 5201A Human Systems Integration Lecture 
TF 7102A Contractor Interaction Lecture
